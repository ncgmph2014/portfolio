One-week MVP project

Astrology App

A non tech summary

An Astrology App where users can find their Sun and Rising signs.

Watch the demo video below

Non technical overview

This was the first react app I created from scratch (other than your run of the mill to-do lists). It was the first app that I created because I wanted to, because I wanted a job at Co-Star Astrology or to make an comparable to what they had but better!!

Technical Overview

In a one-week Sprint, I developed an MVP (Minimum Viable Product) with 2 features--sun sign and rising sign retrieval. I had so much fun creating this app.

Features

MVPs usually require adding one feature, but I added more than that.

  1. Users could add their birthdate and birth times
  2. Users could retrieve their sun sign (the sign that most people already know)
  3. Users could retrive their rising sign (which requires knowing your birth time and is not something everyone knows)
  4. Upon pressing the submit button users received a description of their sun sign

Challenges

So creating this app helped me to develop a lot more respect for astrologers--even though astrology was already a hobby of mine. My goal was actually to be able to retrieve the full chart of each user, which includes the sun and the rising sign (which I was able to caculate on my own) the moon, along with all the planets including Pluto.

This goal turned out to be challenging to achieve.

In order to calculate this, I would have needed an API which costs a lot more money than I had access to or using an ephemeris which is available in Farmer's Almanacs. But entering the data for millions of birth times and places would have required more time than I have left on this earth--it already required about 400 lines of code to calculate the sun and rising signs. In future, I would love to have access to an ephemeris API or utilize an equation that could retrieve the placement of the users planets utilizing the longitude, latitute, and time and date of the user's birth. I would also love to figure out how to shorten the code to fewer lines of code.

Another important issue to add is the way I calculated the rising sign was my using a chart that I could online. Rising signs ideally change every 2 hours. However, the further you were born from the equator, the less this becomes true. Certain parts of the world, signs do last in the rising sign for about two hours. Some stay for three. Some stary for 1. Therefore, the app was not 100% accurate when calculating the rising sign. In the future, access to an ephemeris API would mitigate these inaccuracies
